# Strategy

- The number one priority of Business Continuity is the preservation of life, health, and safety.

The Business Continuity Strategy is:

- Reduce the risks to availability of systems and data whilst maintaining integrity and confidentiality of data.

- Understand business impact of impacts of availability as documented in the Business Impact Analysis

- Understand the prioritisation and recovery goals for people, systems, and locations as document in the Business Impact Analysis

- To have a documented and tested business continuity plan and appropriate, associated disaster recovery plans.

## Standards as applied to Business Continuity.

Business Continuity is aligned with the requirements of ISO 27001 and ISO 22301.

## Objectives

The Business Continuity Objectives are:

- Preservation of life, health, safety, and wellbeing of all

- Minimise the financial impact of any lack of availability of people, systems, premises, or data.

- To maintain all legal and regulatory responsibility

- Minimise the customer impact of any lack of availability of people, systems, premises, or data.

## What will be done?

- A business continuity plan will be in place based on the business impact analysis and needs of the company and tested at least annually.

- Appropriate disaster recovery plans will be in place based on the business impact analysis and needs of the company and tested at least annually.

## What resources will be required?

Resources required as per the business continuity plans and disaster recovery plans in place based on the business impact analysis and needs of the company.

## Who will be responsible?

Responsibility for business continuity and disaster recovery sits with the senior management team. The oversight of the operational elements of the business continuity and disaster recovery have been delegated to the Management Review Team.

## When will it be completed?

Business continuity and disaster recovery is an ongoing process and subject to the continual improvement policy and processes of the business.

## How will results be evaluated?

The effectiveness of the business continuity plan and disaster recovery plans is measured via annual testing with the results being reported to the Management Review Team. As part of the continual improvement policy and process and continual improvements are managed and implemented in line with that process.

Internal audits are conducted on an annual basis that audit against industry best practice and the requirements of ISO 27001 and these are reported to the Management Review Team and subject to the continual improvement policy and process.

External audits are conduct in line with held certifications and customer audit requirements and these are reported to the Management Review Team and subject to the continual improvement policy and process.

## Business Continuity Strategy People

- The high-level people strategy is to ensure the preservation of life, health, safety, and wellbeing of all.

- Effective people management will seek to remove or minimise the impact of key individuals and single points of knowledge.

- Processes and procedures will be documented to remove the reliance on key individuals.

## Business Continuity Strategy Systems

- The high-level systems strategy is to ensure that adequate redundancy and failover is built in by design.

- Based on the business impact analysis and criticality of systems the back-up policy and back up procedures apply.

- Where appropriate a buy not build approach is taken unless specific to our proprietary developed systems.

- Based on the business impact analysis and criticality of disaster recovery plans are in place and tested in support of the business impact assessment.

## Business Continuity Strategy Premises

- The high-level premises strategy is to ensure that staff can work from remote locations.

For hosted environments, the business will seek to deploy into industry standard data centres and solutions and through the Supplier Management Policy and processes seek assurances that these solutions meet industry certifications such as ISO 27001 or SOC with certifications in good standing and appropriate to the services the company consumes.

## Business Continuity Strategy Data

Based on the business impact analysis and criticality of data the back-up policy and back up procedures apply.

## Business Continuity Strategy Suppliers

The Supplier Management Policy and processes seek assurances that suppliers meet industry certifications such as ISO 27001 or SOC with certifications in good standing and appropriate to the services the company consumes.

Suppliers are prioritised based on criticality to the business and managed where appropriate through the risk management policy and processes.

## Business Continuity Strategy Communication

Effective communications are managed when a Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery event occurs.

Third party specialist resource may be sought to help with the communication plan and associated PR based on the event and the decision sits with the Crisis Management Team.

## Management and Operation of Business Continuity

- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery forms part of the organisations Incident Management processes.

- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery can be invoked by any member of the senior management team.

- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery is managed by the Crisis Management Team that convenes on the invocation of Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery. This team oversees operations until restoration.

- Plans and documentation are reviewed annually, subject to internal audit, subject to continual improvement and updated annually or when significant change occurs.
