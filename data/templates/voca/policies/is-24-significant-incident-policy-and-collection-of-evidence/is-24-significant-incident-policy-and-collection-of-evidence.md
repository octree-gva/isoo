# Significant Incident and Collection of Evidence Policy

## Purpose

The document provides the guidance on handling significant incidents in a structured manner including the identification, collection, acquisition, and preservation of information, which can serve as evidence.

## Scope

- All employees and third-party users.

## Principle

Significant incidents and / or incidents that require the collection of evidence require the help of specialist third parties under supervision and management of the organisation.

## Significant Incidents

Significant incidents are defined as incidents that are a legal or regulatory breach that could lead to an external investigation and / or legal proceedings.

## High Level Guidance

- In all instances where a situation may lead to an external investigation and / or legal proceedings specialist external resource must be engaged to carry out the actions.

At the earliest opportunity all work on, access to, modification of, tampering or accessing of the affected and / or in scope systems, documents, locations, files, databases, applications, or other such entities as can be clearly defined must stop. The only exceptions are the preservation of life, health, and safety or to carry out the bare minimum to triage and make safe.

## Process

- Appoint the Significant Incident Team Leader from the senior management team or the SMT as the single point of contact and co-ordination.

- Follow the guidance outlined above to stop and make safe.

- Immediately contact legal representation either from the company legal counsel or authorised, regulated law firms. EXAMPLE: Example Law Firm, 1 High Street, London

- Immediately contact a computer forensic and investigative supplier from a trained and authorised company. EXAMPLE: Example Law Firm, 1 High Street, London

- As required contact the authorities including law enforcement and in country regulator for Data Protection and Industry Specific Regulators.

- If Cyber Security Insurance cover is held immediately inform the Insurance Company.

Follow the guidance of legal counsel, law enforcement and computer forensic investigators and Insurance companies following where possible the incident management processes for recording, tracking, and managing incidents.

## Policy Compliance

- SAMPLE Compliance reviewed annually via internal audit, management review, and recorded staff acknowledgements.

## Compliance Measurement

The information security management team will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

## Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Manager in advance and reported to the Management Review Team.

## Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

## Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
